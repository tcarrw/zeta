Abstract

We explore the connection between divergent series in mathematics, their finite assignments through zeta-regularization, and the τ-framework for unifying time, mass, and energy. Just as the series 1 + 2 + 3 + … can be assigned the value −1/12, the τ framework suggests that infinities in physical theory may yield consistent, finite invariants when recast in temporal–energetic form. We propose tests specifically for zeta-regularization in physics, focusing on vacuum energy and cosmology.

1. Background

In mathematics, divergent series like 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + … do not converge in the traditional sense. Yet, through analytic continuation of the Riemann zeta function ζ(s), the series can be assigned a value of ζ(−1) = −1/12. In physics, such assignments appear in quantum field theory, string theory, and the Casimir effect.

2. Zeta-Regularization

2.1 Riemann ζ function

ζ(s) = ∑_{n=1}^∞ n^{−s}, Re(s) > 1

Through analytic continuation, ζ(s) is extended to the complex plane, except at s = 1 where it diverges.

2.2 Famous example

1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + … = ζ(−1) = −1/12

This is not ordinary summation but a consistent reassignment that preserves analytic structure.

3. τ-Framework Interpretation

In the τ framework, we define τ = m/c = E/c³. Divergences in mass–energy sums can be reframed as divergences in τ. If infinite sums of modes (as in QFT) are rewritten in τ-units, the invariant finite value can be interpreted as a “renormalized τ.”

E_div = ∑_{n=1}^∞ n → τ_eff = (E_div)/c³ = −1/(12 c³)

This suggests τ can serve as a natural regulator: divergent energy sums collapse into finite τ-invariants, analogous to zeta-regularization.

4. Zeta-Specific Tests

4.1 Casimir Effect

Vacuum energy difference between plates already uses ζ(−1). Measuring Casimir forces at varying separations provides a direct laboratory check of zeta-regularized sums.

4.2 String Spectrum Sums

In string theory, infinite mode sums are regularized with ζ(−1). τ-based reformulation can be tested against consistency of anomaly cancellation.

4.3 Cosmological Constant

Vacuum energy density ρ ∼ ∑ ½ ħω is divergent. Rewriting ω = (c³/ħ)τ and applying ζ-regularization provides a finite τ-effective energy density. Compare with observed dark energy density.

5. Implications

  • Zeta-regularization links mathematics of divergence with τ-based renormalization.
  • Casimir effect, string spectra, and dark energy all provide test cases.
  • Suggests τ is not only a bridge between time, mass, and energy but also a regulator of infinities.

A.1 Cesàro vs ζ-summation

Series 1 − 1 + 1 − 1 + … has partial sums oscillating {1, 0, 1, 0,…}. Cesàro summation assigns it ½.

A.2 ζ(−1)

A.3 τ reinterpretation

Define E = c³τ. Then divergent energy sums map to τ-sums:

∑ n → τ_eff = −1/(12c³)
